About

My Name is Lavender Irons, I am an user experience reseacher.

I obtained a bachelor's in psychology to understand the behaviors and motivations of individuals. I worked as a social worker in various capacities; child abuse and neglect, mental health issues in children and adults, and social issues. Deciding to work more with children, I obtained a master's in education. I taught for a few years, then transition to an administrator at a university.

For career development and to expand my knowledge, I completed classes in user research and technology. I strive to gain insight into people’s actions. I am persistent and systematic in solving problems and have a passion for learning.

I apply past knowledge and skills to qualitive and quantitative research methods. Qualitative methods I have utilize include case studies, focus groups, interviews, and additional information gathering methods. I also have experience with quantitative methods, such as; questionnaires, surveys, observations, and data analysis.

I am currently exploring doctorate programs in human-computer interaction.

Experience

Redesign Alternative Blog

This alternative blog is associated with a clothing line, which is their main focus. The blog was set up to promote the clothes. After a review, it was established that several items needed to be changed. Through various user interviews, surveys, and prototypes, I was able to determine the updates.

Updated Features of Pizza Website

Using the pizza website I notice two items, if added, would allow for a better user experience. First, saving the location site. The second, when using car side delivery, being able to save color, make, model of the car and preferred location where you would like them to leave the order. Through usability testing, I discovered many users who stated that they would utilize the save button and it would expedite placing their orders. Based on my research findings, I designed solutions and validated these changes with additional usability tests.

Landing Page Redesign

Having a landing page should be clear, concise, and brief. This landing page is extremely long, it’s formatted like a newspaper and reads like a late night commercial. The items need to be arranged on a separate page. User interviews stated that they were overwhelmed with all of the information listed. Presenting the low-def prototypes the users stated items that needed to be change. Hi-def prototypes made these adjustments and were presented to stakeholders.
